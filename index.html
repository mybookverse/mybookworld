🧳 the slow travel manifesto

We believe in spending at least five nights in one place. Learning a few words of the language. Shopping at mercatos, not souvenir stalls. Taking the train that arrives at midnight. Drift & Root is an independent magazine and community founded in 2022. We don't take sponsorships from airlines or hotel chains. Our guides are written by locals and long‑term travelers — people who know the difference between tourist traps and family‑run bakeries. We cover everything from temple stay etiquette in Japan to which regional olive oil to look for in Puglia. Our Morocco guide was written after three months living in Fes; the Kyoto guide is updated seasonally by Mika, a Kyoto‑based writer. We also host monthly virtual "tea talks" — next one: train travel in Central Asia with special guest Elena from Tashkent. All content is ad‑free, supported by readers like you. ⛩️ Kyoto · 京都

words by Mika Tanaka · 8‑day slow itinerary · updated april 2026

🍵 essentials

  • stay: Guesthouse "Kyo no yado" (Nishijin district) · ¥8,500/night
  • ritual: 6am meditation at Kennin-ji (zazen)
  • meal: Shojin ryori at Izusen · ¥3,800
  • craft: Kintsugi workshop with Daimon-san · ¥6,500
  • transit: Bus pass + rental bicycle · ¥1,200/day

≈ €62

local tip: "arigatai" — a feeling of grateful rarity.

📿 notes from the path

Most visitors race between temples. Instead, we sat in one garden for two hours. The moss garden at Saiho-ji is best in late May – reservations are essential, and our guide explains how to book. For food: look for "itamae" (chef-owned) counter spots. We loved a small tofu place near Demachi‑yanagi, open since 1978. Day trip: Uji for matcha and the Phoenix Hall (Byodoin). Avoid busiest hours (10am–2pm) at Kinkaku-ji. Our full Kyoto zine includes 14 hand‑drawn maps and a foldable itinerary. Also: we recommend the Ōhara region, one hour north – Sanzen-in temple and rural farming stays. We've partnered with a local cooperative for homestays; proceeds support temple preservation.

seasonal: autumn leaves at Tofuku-ji (mid‑November). spring booking opens january.

🎎 interview: kimono restorer

"We use threads from old kimono to repair temple scrolls. Slow fashion, slow faith." – Sato-san, 35 years in Nishijin.

🌾 Puglia · il tacco

by Chiara Bellini · spring itinerary · 6 nights

🥨 salento highlights

  • Trullo Sovrano, Alberobello
  • pasta workshop with Nonna Rosa (€45)
  • Ostuni at golden hour
  • swimming in Polignano a Mare
  • olive oil tasting, Masseria Brancati
  • Alberobello by night (tourist‑free)

🚲 slow travel notes

Rent a bike in Monopoli, cycle along the coast to Egnazia ruins (12km flat). Avoid August – we go in May or September. Eat orecchiette with cime di rapa, drink Primitivo, buy almonds from the market in Martina Franca. Puglia is also about the people: learn "buongiorno" with a smile. Our local cooperative runs agriturismo stays in converted trulli – €70/night includes breakfast and dinner with wine. We also recommend the remote Tremiti islands for a two‑day escape. Chiara’s guide includes 8 hand‑drawn maps and a glossary of dialect food terms. Also: look for "fave e cicoria" – a rustic bean puree.

spring event: Alberobello's "Trulli in Fiore" – first weekend of May.

🐪 Morocco · beyond the souks

by Karim Elalami · 12-day slow circuit · Fes – Atlas – Sahara

🏜️ desert & mountains

  • Berber homestay, Imlil valley
  • Sunset camel trek, Merzouga
  • Fes tanneries (early morning)
  • Volubilis ruins
  • Mint tea with artisans, Meknes

📖 slow Morocco

This is not a whirlwind tour. We suggest 3 nights in Fes, 2 nights in the Atlas with a Berber family, and 3 nights in the desert – including a night at a camp without electricity. Karim’s family runs a guesthouse in the Dades Valley. Learn to cook tagine, walk through palm groves, and listen to Gnawa music. Avoid Marrakech if you dislike crowds; we include alternatives like Sefrou. Our Morocco guide has been field‑updated in February 2026. Also: train from Fes to Meknes is 30 min and costs €2.50. We support the AMREC association for Amazigh language preservation.
